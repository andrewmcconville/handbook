In Part 9, Documenting, you learn differing academic conventions for citing sources in your writing. You also learn how to create the lists (that go at the end of every academic paper) of expected information about your sources .

In this part you see the final drafts of papers in MLA and APA style.You see how these papers’ writers used their writing to “discover and use ideas” (to use the words of the WPA Outcomes). You also see how writers can use reflection to help them learn.

Academic English’s conventions for the use of sources imply respect for the sources. Learning the conventions of in-text and end-of-paper citations helps you use sources respectfully and with confidence.
