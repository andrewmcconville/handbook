In Part 6, Drafting a Paper, Connecting with Audiences, you learn how to think about your writing purposes by thinking about the specific audiences you hope to engage. You also learn how to think about composing a draft of your paper as part of the process of figuring out what matters to you in your writing.

On the following screens, you learn strategies for engaging rhetorically with audiences before you write. You also learn how to see the various aspects of writing—voice, tone, level of formality, and organization—in a draft.

As you draft for an audience, you continually question how your ideas connect and reach others.

Drafting a paper puts you closer to a finished paper—but also enables you to figure out what really matters to you in your writing, a necessary part of a composing process. Seeing a draft in process helps you see where revision is necessary—and allows others to give you feedback, which you need to learn how to request and how to use. All these parts of a composing process engage you with the social and collaborative aspects of all writing.
