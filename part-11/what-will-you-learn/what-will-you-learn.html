In this part, you learn the final steps for polishing your writing according to academic conventions. This part helps you make your writing be as understandable as possible for your readers.

The WPA Outcomes say that writers should develop "facility in responding to a variety of situations and contexts calling for purposeful shifts in voice, tone, level of formality, design, medium, and/or structure."

Learning about grammar, punctuation, and mechanics helps you make such purposeful shifts to address an audience’s expectations about voice, tone, and level of formality.

Writers generally attend to grammar, punctuation, and mechanics as the last step in a composing processes, once the writing is steady and calls for no more revision.

The WPA Outcomes Statement says that "Conventions are the formal rules and informal guidelines that define genres, and in so doing, shape readers’ and writers’ perceptions of correctness or appropriateness. Most obviously, conventions govern such things as mechanics, usage, spelling, and citation practices."

The Outcomes Statement also says that "Successful writers understand, analyze, and negotiate conventions for purpose, audience, and genre, understanding that genres evolve in response to changes in material conditions and composing technologies and attending carefully to emergent conventions."

In this part of the Interactive Handbook, you can learn and practice the conventions of Academic English Writing in the United States.
