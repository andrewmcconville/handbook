Commas, you will learn, have four main uses:

  • 1

    to make numbers, place-names, and dates clear

    For Example:

    Virginia’s population was 1,000,000 in 1830. It took eighty more years for it to reach its second million (2,061,612 in 1910).

    Abraham Lincoln was shot the night of April 14, 1865, and died the following morning.

  • 2

    when quoting others’ words

    For Example:

    Muhammad Ali once said, “I am the astronaut of boxing. Joe Louis and Dempsey were just jet pilots.”

    “Hello,” she said, “can I help you with that?”

    “The real problem with having a robot to dinner,” argues Ellen Ullman, “is pleasure.”

  • 3

    to separate words in lists

    For Example:

    She caught a cab, her breath, and then the flu.

    He was livid, he was angry, and he was mad.

    Nowadays, no one has friends and neighbors, what with cellphones, radio, television, and computers.

  • 4

    to build sentences that contain multiple parts

    For Example:

    Her father, who was born in Saudi Arabia, always longed for the hottest days in August.

    You’d think it would be enough that she earned A’s in all her classes, but my roommate, a biomechanical engineering major, also wanted to have the highest GPA on campus.

    He looked up at me, and he burst into tears.
