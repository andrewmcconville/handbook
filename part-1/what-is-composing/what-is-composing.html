What is Composing?

Composing a paper, you arrange words into sentences into paragraphs, hoping the arrangement will engage your readers. Composing a poster, you arrange words and photographs and colors, hoping viewers will want to look at the poster and heed its purposes. Composing a podcast, you arrange voices and other sounds, hoping others want to listen. In other words, composing—as we use it in this book—refers to written, visual, oral, and mixed-media texts that you create.
