Research paper assignment

Over the term you will produce an argumentative research paper on a topic of interest to you.

We will discuss “argument” as we proceed, so that you will develop an understanding of the characteristics of—and work that goes into—argumentative writing.

Details of the paper

  • The audience for your writing is your classmates.
  • The paper will be 5–7 pages long.
  • You will cite at least six sources in your writing, four of which must be academic sources.
  • Based on your major, you will choose either MLA or APA style for your citations and Works Cited list or References list.

Objectives

I ask you to do this work to help you:

  • learn how to find and use authoritative and relevant sources.
  • develop your critical thinking abilities through analyzing your sources’ arguments.
  • learn how to support and develop your ideas through engaging with your sources’ arguments.
  • learn scholarly conventions for writing.

Seeing purpose

To understand what a teacher hopes you learn, look for these terms:

  • summarize: to describe as concisely as possible the main points of writing
  • define: to explain a term or concept
  • inform: to tell others about an issue, with supporting examples and data
  • analyze: to break a process or object into its parts
  • persuade/argue: to present your opinions on a topic using evidence, so that others might come to agreement with you

Seeing audience

Look in the assignment for a description of an audience; if you do not see a description, ask.

Note that, in an assignment, the audience description is often general; as you develop an assignment, you learn the thoughts, beliefs, feelings, and experiences that this general audience holds that matter to the specific purposes of your writing.

Seeing context

Look in the assignment for a description of context—but recognize that an audience description might imply an initial context, as in this assignment.

This assignment initially places your work within your classroom and school, at this moment. Your further development of your purpose and audience will help you fine-tune the context.
