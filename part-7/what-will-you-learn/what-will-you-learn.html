Part 7, Revising with Style, engages you with what some find to be the most pleasurable parts of writing: having figured out your ideas through drafting, you can really shape your writing for others, experimenting with words and phrases and moving around the parts of your writing.

Once you’ve drafted a paper whose argument gives you satisfaction, digging into shaping your writing carefully for others can be a joy. You experiment with voice, tone, level of formality, design, and structur, all in the service if engaging your readers.

Revising ripens writing, helping you move from the earlier steps of a writing process toward the final polishing… although you might need to revisit earlier steps to find new ideas or answer new questions. Importantly, you need to learn to revise in response to your particular rhetorical situation, staying flexible and open to what you learn from your readers.

In revising for your particular audience, you learn to pay attention to the conventions you know and the conventions you need to learn better.
