In Part 10, Editing and Proofreading Your Work, you learn the differences among revising, editing, and proofreading. Knowing these differences helps you understand where to focus your energies at differing steps in your writing process.

While editing and proofreading sometimes seem to be all about rules, they are rhetorical: how do you shape your grammar for your particular audience?

Editing and proofreading are usually the final steps of a writing process. For most writers, it only makes sense to edit and proofread once all your ideas are in place and you know there are no big changes coming.

Learning the expectations of academic writing helps you learn possibilities for shaping your writing for different audiences and their expectations about convention.
