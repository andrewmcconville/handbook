Part 8, Academic English, focuses on helping you learn and make solid use of the conventions of academic writing in English. This part can be of particular use to you if you speak and write in multiple languages, but is helpful for anyone for whom academic writing has its quirks.

Part 8 helps you address what the WPA Outcomes describes as central to becoming a strong, fluent, confident writer: developing “facility in responding to a variety of situations and contexts calling for purposeful shifts in voice, tone, level of formality, design, medium, and/or structure,” by developing facility in working within academic conventions.

Part 8 continues to help you develop strategies for drafting, reviewing, revising, rewriting, and editing your work.

Academic English writing has many conventions. Part 8 helps you learn these conventions and so to (using the words of the WPA Outcomes) “develop knowledge of linguistic structures, including grammar, punctuation, and spelling, through practice in composing and revising.” This part will also help you “understand why genre conventions for structure, paragraphing, tone, and mechanics vary” and will help you “gain experience negotiating variations in genre conventions.”
