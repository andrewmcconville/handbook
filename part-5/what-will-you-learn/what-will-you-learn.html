Part 5, Organizing and Shaping Texts,offers you strategies for linking your writing purposes with how you organize your writing.

In Part 5, you learn how to think rhetorically about organizing your writing to shape it for your purposes and for different audiences.

In Part 5, you learn about patterns of organization writers can use—which can help you in your own writing as well as in reading and understanding others’ writing.

On the following screens you will learn why genre conventions for structure and paragraphing shift across texts for differing audiences. You will also learn conventional structures for paragraphs—and for visual and oral texts.
