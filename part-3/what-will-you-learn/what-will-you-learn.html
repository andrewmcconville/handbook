With Part 3, Evaluating and Tracking Sources Ethically, you learn more about the integrity academic writers have toward the sources from which they draw their ideas and information. First, how do you evaluate sources for their relevance and credibility, so that you choose the sources appropriate for your audiences and purposes? Second, how do you keep track of your sources, so that you use them with the respect their authors deserve, by citing them following academic conventions?

The WPA Outcomes Statements acknowledge that writers need to be evaluate their sources “for credibility, sufficiency, accuracy, timeliness, bias and so on,” and in this part of the Interactive Handbook you learn to do that.

In Part 3, we address two items from the WPA Outcomes Statement about conventions:

You will learn about intellectual property (including about fair use and copyright) and how the notion of intellectual property shapes academic approaches to sources.

You will learn how to record the information about your sources that is necessary for citing your sources following academic conventions.
